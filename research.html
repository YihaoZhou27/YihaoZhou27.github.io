Research Highlights

Selected projects and their key findings

2025

The ASTRID Simulation at z=0

ASTRID is one of the largest hydrodynamical cosmological simulations evolved to $z=0$. It hosts $2\times5500^3$ particles in a box size of 250 Mpc/h per side. ASTRID features a large population of massive black holes (MBHs), ranging from $10^4$ to $10^{11}$ M$_\odot$, and implements a subgrid dynamical friction model to provide a better description of the MBH orbital evolution. This makes it a powerful tool to study the formation and evolution of MBHs in a cosmological context.

2024

MAGICS: Massive black hole Assembly in Galaxies Informed by Cosmological Simulations

To bridge the gap between cosmological simulations and small-scale MBH dynamics, we developed MAGICS. We reproduce merging systems in ASTRID with high-resolution idealized simulations, and implement better gravity solver: regularized integrator KETJU and $N$-body code TAICHI. This allows us to study the MBH dynamics on sub-parsec scales, which gives a better description of the MBH merger process.

2024

PTA Continuous Wave Sources

We uses the MBH population in ASTRID to predict the continuous wave (CW) sources for the PTA experiments. We found all the high-detectability CW sources are from the central galaxies of the massive galaxy clusters.

2023

Predicting LISA Detection Rates

Based on ASTRID, we predicted the detection rates of the LISA experiment. We found that considering the evolution of MBH orbital eccentricity, the black hole mass range explored by LISA is extended to high mass end: $\approx 10^9$ M$_\odot$.
